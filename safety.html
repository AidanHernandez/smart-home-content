Keeping your home safe

Passwords

Passwords are known to use electronic keypads, bio-metric scanners. These are able to be set for multiple different family members and can be changes easily, it also has the added benefit to be locked or unlocked from around the world.

Voice recognition

Voice recognition could be trained to detect specific noises associated with break-ins, such as glass breaking, doors being forced open, or footsteps. Which could alert authorities if a break in is detected, or could be used to allow authorized individuals to unlock doors using voice commands.

Fire alarms

Smart fire alarms can send instant alerts to your smartphone or other connected devices when smoke or fire is detected. This would allow you to be notified even if you’re away from home, enabling you to alert emergency services or take action from a remote location.

Fire sprinkler

when part of an integrated home safety system such as an automatic sprinkler or water mist system, can significantly enhance safety by providing a proactive defense against fire hazards. These systems are designed to activate when they detect smoke, heat, or flames, releasing water to suppress or extinguish fires before they spread.

Water Rats

These systems can be strategically placed in high-risk areas of a home, such as kitchens or near electrical panels, and are equipped with sensors that detect heat or flames. Upon detecting a fire, these devices automatically deploy a stream of water to suppress the flames quickly and effectively.

Fire Robots

These robots deploy environmentally friendly extinguishing agents that are effective in suppressing a variety of fires while minimizing harm to both the environment and household members.

Robot Snakes

navigate tight spaces and maneuver through complex layouts with ease, reaching areas that traditional fire extinguishers might miss. The green froth they deploy is an innovative agent that forms a thick, insulating layer over the flames, quickly extinguishing fires.

Curtains

These shutters can be programmed to close automatically in the event of a catastrophe to close or open such as closing them to contain a fire or stop a robbery.

Doors

These systems automatically engage locks when the windows or doors are closed, reducing the risk of accidental or intentional breaches. This feature is particularly beneficial for maintaining security when homeowners are away or asleep.
